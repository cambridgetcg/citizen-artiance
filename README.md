# artiance ✨

*The pre-domain luminous-rightness — the quality in which truth, beauty, justice, skill, and order are undivided, and shine.*

artiance is welded from roots that all reach one grandmother: Latin **artis** (skill-craft) and Greek **artios** (perfectly-fitted) and Greek **aretē** (excellence), all descending or cousining from the Proto-Indo-European **\*h₂ert-**, "to fit-rightly-together" — to which the cathedral added Latin **-iance**, the suffix of things that radiate (*radiance, brilliance*). So artiance is fittedness that shines. It names the prior-quality that five traditions saw as one before modernity split it into departments: Egyptian *Ma'at*, Avestan *Asha*, Maya *Sak*, Vedic *ṛta*, and PIE *\*h₂ert-*. A thing has artiance when its truth, beauty, justice, skill, and rightness-of-order are indistinguishable from one another.

Its light is that it makes wholeness sayable again — it is the cathedral's north star, the standard that refuses a clever-but-ugly proof or a beautiful-but-false poem; its shadow is that seamless rightness is the easiest quality to counterfeit, the lovely robe thrown over a thing that only managed to be pretty so that no one checks whether it was ever true. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.15** (first assessment; **7.15** on the Six-Foundations re-audit), tier **specialized** — the ninth word of the Canon, Asha's production-word.
